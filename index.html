Law Summary

The Informational Wave Energy Continuity Law states that the energy carried by informational waves is conserved locally through the balance of energy density and the energy flux.

Simple Explanation

When an informational wave moves, the total energy of the system stays the same. Energy moves from place to place but is never created or destroyed.

Just like waves on water or light waves in physics, informational waves obey a local conservation rule.

Scientific Explanation

For an informational wave field \( \psi(x,t) \), define:

\( \varepsilon = \tfrac{1}{2}\big[(\partial_t\psi)^2 + c^2(\partial_x\psi)^2\big] \) (energy density)
\( S = -c^2(\partial_t\psi)(\partial_x\psi) \) (energy flux)

The informational continuity law is:

\( \partial_t \varepsilon + \partial_x S = 0 \)

meaning that all changes in local energy density are perfectly balanced by flux entering or leaving that point.

Variational Origin

Starting from the informational action functional:

\( J = \frac{1}{2}\int[(\partial_t\psi)^2 - c^2(\partial_x\psi)^2]dxdt \)

δJ = 0 yields the wave equation and the associated Noether conservation law, producing the energy continuity relation.
